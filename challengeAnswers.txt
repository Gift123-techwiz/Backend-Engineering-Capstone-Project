Question 1
Why is storing plain passwords dangerous even in small applications?
Answer => Storing plain passwords is dangerous because it enables hackers to easily have access to users accounts.


Question 2
What is the difference between authentication and authorization. Give real-world examples.
Answer => Authentication has to do with proving who a user is while authentication has to do with what certain users can access.
          For example A user loggin in to an account on a platform, authentication checks who the person is and whether the person is among its users.
          After confirming who the person is authorization then comes in. Like in a business a secretary can delete other users but a users doesn't have the access to delete any user.
          or a teacher, as the admin, having access to edit the scores of students while students can't edit scores.All the students has access to is viewing his scores.


Question 3
Why is JWT expiration important?
What could happen if tokens never expire?
Answer =>  JWT expiration is important because it makes platforms/sites/webpages/user accounts more secure and safe from hackers.
           If tokens never expire and it ends up stolen by a hacker, the hacker will have access to users accounts/info indefinitely


Question 4
A hacker gets access to a valid JWT token.
What are 3 things you can implement to reduce damage?
Answer => a. Force a log out; b. Block any form of authorization until the token expires; c. Monitor accounts
